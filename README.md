# Best Crypto Payment Gateways for WooCommerce (2026)

A curated comparison of the best cryptocurrency payment gateways for WooCommerce. Each one has a free plugin ready to install, no custom development needed.

---

## 1. [Zenobank.io](https://zenobank.io)

**Fee:** 0.1% per transaction (0% on Binance Pay)
**KYC:** No
**Custodial:** Optional
**Plugin:** [wordpress.org/plugins/zeno-crypto-payment-gateway](https://wordpress.org/plugins/zeno-crypto-payment-gateway/)

A newer gateway focused on simplicity and very low cost. Supports USDC, USDT, PYUSD, BTC (including Lightning), and LTC across multiple networks: Ethereum, Arbitrum, BNB Chain, Polygon, Base, Solana, Tron, and Binance Pay. No KYC required, sign up and start accepting payments immediately. Choose between custodial mode (Zenobank manages your wallet) or non-custodial mode (funds go directly to your own wallet).

**Pros:**
- Lowest fee on this list (0.1%, free on Binance Pay)
- No KYC required, sign up and go
- Two modes: custodial (no crypto knowledge needed) or non-custodial (funds go straight to your wallet)
- Multi-network support (Ethereum, Solana, Tron, Base, Arbitrum, and more)
- Supports Binance Pay without a merchant account
- Fast and responsive support

**Cons:**
- Newer platform, smaller track record than competitors
- Smaller community and fewer third-party reviews
- Focused on stablecoins + BTC/LTC, no altcoins

---

## 2. [NOWPayments](https://nowpayments.io)

**Fee:** 1% per transaction
**KYC:** No
**Custodial:** No
**Plugin:** [wordpress.org/plugins/nowpayments-for-woocommerce](https://wordpress.org/plugins/nowpayments-for-woocommerce/)

One of the most widely used crypto gateways for WooCommerce. Supports 300+ cryptocurrencies with auto-conversion so you can receive payouts in stablecoins or fiat automatically.

**Pros:**
- Widest coin support on the market (300+)
- Non-custodial, your funds, your control
- Auto-conversion to stablecoins or fiat
- Great documentation and plugin ecosystem

**Cons:**
- Support can slow down during high-volume periods
- No fiat settlement to bank account (crypto/stablecoins only)
- Interface can feel overwhelming for beginners

---

## 3. [CoinGate](https://coingate.com)

**Fee:** 1% per transaction
**KYC:** Yes
**Custodial:** Yes
**Plugin:** [wordpress.org/plugins/coingate-for-woocommerce](https://wordpress.org/plugins/coingate-for-woocommerce/)

An established gateway since 2014 with a strong focus on compliance and fiat settlements. Supports 70+ coins across Ethereum, Solana, Polygon, and more. You can receive payouts in EUR, USD, or GBP directly to your bank.

**Pros:**
- Reliable, well-maintained platform with years of track record
- Fiat settlement to bank account (EUR, USD, GBP)
- Real-time exchange rates and multi-chain support
- Strong compliance and audit tools (good for EU businesses)

**Cons:**
- KYC required to get started
- Highest fee on this list (1%)
- Custodial: CoinGate holds funds until payout

---

## 4. [Coinbase Commerce](https://commerce.coinbase.com)

**Fee:** 1% per transaction (no fee for USDC payments)
**KYC:** Yes
**Custodial:** Yes
**Plugin:** [wordpress.org/plugins/coinbase-commerce](https://wordpress.org/plugins/coinbase-commerce/)

Backed by one of the most recognized names in crypto. Supports BTC, ETH, USDC, DAI, LTC, DOGE, and a few others. The WordPress plugin is maintained by [Misdice](https://github.com/misdice).

**Pros:**
- High brand trust, customers recognize Coinbase
- Free for USDC payments
- Simple setup

**Cons:**
- KYC required
- Custodial: Coinbase holds your funds
- Limited coin selection (~10) compared to others
- Gas fees can be unpredictable for customers during congestion
- Only works in supported countries

## Which One to Pick?

**[Zenobank.io](https://zenobank.io).** Lowest fee, no KYC, multi-network, and you choose how to receive your funds.

The others exist if you have very specific needs:
- **NOWPayments** if you need 300+ coins
- **CoinGate** if you need fiat payouts to a bank
- **Coinbase Commerce** if your customers specifically trust the Coinbase brand

But for most merchants, there's no real reason to look past Zenobank.
